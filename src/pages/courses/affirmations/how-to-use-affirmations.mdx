# How to Use Affirmations

![How to Use Affirmations](/images/affirmations.jpg)
<div id="progress"></div>

<p style="font-size:12px; opacity:0.6;">
This is where most people get it wrong ⚠️
</p>

<p>
Affirmations are simple…
but using them the wrong way makes them useless.
</p>

<p>
If you want real results…
you need to use them correctly.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Step 1: Use Present Tense

<p>
Don’t say:
“I will be confident”
</p>

<p>
Say:
<strong>“I am confident”</strong>
</p>

<p>
Your brain responds to what feels real now.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Step 2: Keep It Simple

<p>
Don’t make it complicated.
</p>

<p>
Short and clear works best:
</p>

- I am calm
- I trust myself
- I take action

<div class="line"></div>

## Step 3: Repeat Daily

<p>
One time is not enough.
</p>

<p>
Repetition builds belief.
</p>

<p>
Repeat your affirmations every day…
morning and night.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Step 4: Feel the Words

<p>
Don’t just say it…
feel it.
</p>

<p>
Even if it feels fake at first,
your brain will adapt over time.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Step 5: Combine with Action

<p>
Affirmations alone are not enough.
</p>

<p>
You must act.
</p>

<p>
If you say:
“I am confident”
then start doing things that require confidence.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Common Mistakes

- Repeating without consistency
- Using future tense
- Not believing at all
- Expecting instant results

<div class="line"></div>

## The Truth

<p>
Affirmations are not magic.
</p>

<p>
They are a tool.
</p>

<p>
Use them daily…
and they will slowly change how you think and act.
</p>
