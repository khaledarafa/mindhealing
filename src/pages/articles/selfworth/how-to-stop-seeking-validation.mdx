# How to Stop Seeking Validation

![How to Stop Seeking Validation](/images/selfworth.jpg)

<p>
Many people depend on validation
to feel worthy.
</p>

<p>
They need approval,
attention,
or praise
to feel good about themselves.
</p>

<p>
But when your value depends on others…
your peace disappears.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Why People Seek Validation

<p>
Validation often comes from insecurity,
fear of rejection,
or low self worth.
</p>

<p>
People want to feel accepted and appreciated.
</p>

<p>
But external approval never feels enough for long.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Step 1: Understand Your Real Value

<p>
Your worth does not increase
when people praise you.
</p>

<p>
And it does not disappear
when someone dislikes you.
</p>

<p>
Your value already exists.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Step 2: Stop Explaining Yourself Constantly

<p>
You do not need permission
for every decision you make.
</p>

<p>
Constantly explaining yourself
often comes from fear of judgment.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Step 3: Build Self Approval

<p>
Ask yourself:
</p>

<ul>
<li>Do I respect myself?</li>
<li>Am I being honest with myself?</li>
<li>Am I improving my life?</li>
</ul>

<p>
Internal approval matters more than external approval.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Step 4: Accept That Not Everyone Will Like You

<p>
No matter what you do,
some people will judge you.
</p>

<p>
That is part of life.
</p>

<p>
Freedom begins
when you stop trying to please everyone.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Step 5: Spend More Time Being Yourself

<p>
The more authentic you become,
the less validation you need.
</p>

<p>
Stop performing for people.
</p>

<p>
Start living for yourself.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Final Thought

<p>
Validation feels good temporarily…
but self worth creates lasting peace.
</p>

<p>
You do not need everyone’s approval
to have value.
</p>

<p>
Respect yourself,
trust yourself,
and keep growing.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

<p>
Want to build stronger self worth and confidence?
</p>
