# Why You Feel Unworthy

![Why You Feel Unworthy](/images/self-worth.jpg)

<div id="progress"></div>

<p style="font-size:12px; opacity:0.6;">
Keep going… you're almost there 🔥
</p>

<p>
After becoming aware of your current state, you might have noticed something uncomfortable…
</p>

<p>
A feeling of lack.
A feeling that you have to struggle just to get what others receive easily.
</p>

<p>
So the real question is:
<strong>Why is your self worth low in the first place?</strong>
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## The Truth

<p>
What you feel inside is not who you truly are…
</p>

<p>
It’s what was programmed into you.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Old Programming

<p>
Since childhood, your mind has been collecting everything.
</p>

<p>
Every word, every reaction, every look, every moment of rejection…
all of it became part of your subconscious programming.
</p>

<p>
For example:
</p>

- If you heard “Nothing comes easy”, you started believing that good things must come with struggle.
- If someone told you “You don’t deserve it”, even as a joke, it stayed inside you.
- If you were told “What did you do to deserve this?”, you started believing you must earn your worth.

<div class="line"></div>

## Negative Beliefs You Grew Up With

<p>
You may still carry beliefs like:
</p>

- Money is hard to get
- Life is unfair
- You must suffer to succeed
- People always leave
- I am not like them
- No one will choose me
- Success is only for lucky people

<p>
These beliefs build invisible walls inside you.
</p>

<p>
Not because good things are not coming…
but because part of you is rejecting them without realizing it.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Painful Experiences

- You may have experienced neglect or rejection
- You may have been denied simple things
- You may have seen struggle around you constantly

<p>
Each painful experience slowly reduced your sense of self worth.
</p>

<p>
Until you reached a point where you feel you must work extremely hard just to receive basic things.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Now What?

<p>
Awareness is the first step.
</p>

<p>
Your low self worth is not your identity…
it’s just programming, experiences, and old fear.
</p>

<p>
And that’s the best news.
</p>

<p>
Because if it’s not who you truly are…
<strong>you can change it.</strong>
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Simple Exercise

<p>
Take a moment.
</p>

<p>
Write down every negative sentence you remember hearing in your life.
</p>

<p>
Even small ones.
Even if they came from people who loved you.
</p>

<p>
Then ask yourself:
</p>

<p>
<strong>Do I still want to believe this… or is it time to let it go?</strong>
</p>
