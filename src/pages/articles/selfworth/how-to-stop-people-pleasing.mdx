# How to Stop People Pleasing

![How to Stop People Pleasing](/images/selfworth.jpg)

<p>
Do you constantly worry about disappointing people?
</p>

<p>
Do you say “yes” even when you want to say “no”?
</p>

<p>
If so…
you may be stuck in people pleasing.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## What Is People Pleasing?

<p>
People pleasing is when you constantly try to make other people happy…
even at the expense of yourself.
</p>

<p>
You avoid conflict.
You fear rejection.
And you feel responsible for other people’s emotions.
</p>

<p>
Over time…
this becomes emotionally exhausting.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Why People Pleasing Happens

<p>
Many people learn people pleasing as a survival pattern.
</p>

<p>
You may have learned that:
</p>

<ul>
<li>Your needs are not important</li>
<li>You must avoid upsetting people</li>
<li>You need approval to feel worthy</li>
<li>Love must be earned</li>
</ul>

<p>
Because of this…
you start abandoning yourself to keep others comfortable.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## Signs You Are a People Pleaser

<ul>
<li>You struggle to say no</li>
<li>You fear disappointing others</li>
<li>You apologize too much</li>
<li>You avoid expressing your real feelings</li>
<li>You feel guilty when setting boundaries</li>
<li>You constantly seek validation</li>
</ul>

<div class="line"></div>

## Why It Damages Self Worth

<p>
People pleasing slowly destroys your relationship with yourself.
</p>

<p>
You stop asking:
</p>

<p>
“What do I need?”
</p>

<p>
And start focusing only on:
</p>

<p>
“What do other people want from me?”
</p>

<p>
Over time…
this creates emotional burnout, resentment, and insecurity.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## How to Stop People Pleasing

<p>
Healing starts with small changes.
</p>

<ul>
<li>Practice saying no without guilt</li>
<li>Stop over explaining yourself</li>
<li>Set healthier boundaries</li>
<li>Accept that not everyone will like you</li>
<li>Notice when you abandon your own needs</li>
</ul>

<p>
At first…
this may feel uncomfortable.
</p>

<p>
But discomfort is part of growth.
</p>

<div class="line"></div>

## You Are Allowed to Protect Your Energy

<p>
You are not selfish for having boundaries.
</p>

<p>
You are not responsible for fixing everyone.
</p>

<p>
And you do not need to earn love by sacrificing yourself.
</p>
